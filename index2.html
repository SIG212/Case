About

Design that works in the real world, not just in Figma.

I'm a UI/UX Designer with 7 years of experience across healthcare, HR tech, and digital services. I work across the full design process — research, interaction design, prototyping, testing, and working closely with developers to make sure things ship the way they were intended.

Before moving into UX, I spent several years in digital marketing. That background gives me a different lens: I think about users, but I also think about why a product exists and what it needs to do for the business.

I find that combination useful — especially in complex domains where the problem is rarely just a UI problem.

  • 01
    Start with the problem
    Good design starts before the first wireframe. I spend time understanding what's actually going wrong before deciding what to build.
  • 02
    Design for the edge case
    The average user flow is easy. The important work is in the moments when things don't go as expected.
  • 03
    Stay close to the build
    I work with developers throughout delivery. A design that doesn't survive implementation isn't a good design.
  • 04
    Show the thinking
    I document decisions, not just outcomes. Understanding why a choice was made is as important as the choice itself.
  • 05
    Embrace the change
    For the past six months I've been using AI heavily across my workflow — research, ideation, prototyping, and writing frontend code. I don't treat it as a shortcut; I treat it as a new material to understand properly.

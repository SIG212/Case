01 — The problem

Getting to the race is half the battle.

Trail races in Romania are usually in the mountains — far from cities, with limited parking and no public transport. Most runners drive alone, or spend hours in Facebook groups trying to coordinate rides with strangers.

I've been part of this community for years. The coordination problem was real and constant — people asking in comments, DMing each other, showing up with half-empty cars while others struggled to get there. There was no dedicated tool for it.

The friction before
  • Ride coordination scattered across Facebook groups
  • No way to find rides for a specific race
  • No structure — just informal DMs and comments
  • Drivers didn't know who needed a ride
  • Last-minute cancellations with no system
What I built
  • Event-based ride listing — one race, one page
  • Offer or request a seat in a few clicks
  • Guest requests without mandatory signup
  • Email notifications via Resend
  • Embeddable widget for race organizers
02 — The product

Built for how runners actually think.

The flow is simple: find your race, see who's driving, request a seat or offer one. No unnecessary steps, no mandatory registration for passengers.

Events list
Browse upcoming races with active ride offers. Filter by date or location.
Ride offers
All drivers for a race in one place — departure point, seats available, contact.
Offer a ride
Drivers post in under a minute — route, seats, departure time.
Request a seat
Guest requests without signup. Driver gets notified by email, confirms directly.

04 — What I learned

Building solo is a different kind of constraint.

1
You make different tradeoffs when you're also the user
I've been to dozens of trail races. That context meant I knew immediately what mattered — guest requests without signup, email over push notifications, departure point over exact address. Domain knowledge shortcuts a lot of research.
2
Supabase RLS is genuinely tricky to get right
Row-level security policies look simple until you have guest users, authenticated users, and admins all hitting the same tables. I debugged more RLS issues than I expected — but came out understanding the pattern properly.
3
An embeddable widget changes the distribution model
Adding a widget that race organizers can embed on their own sites was the biggest unlock — it puts the product where the runners already are, instead of asking them to come to a new platform.
